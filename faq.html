FAQs

Have you Question?we are here to help.

Q1:How far in advance should I book hotel rooms for my wedding guests?

It's best to book hotel rooms for your wedding guests as early as possible, ideally six to twelve months in advance. This allows your guests to secure their accommodations and ensures availability at your preferred hotels.


Q2:Can I negotiate special rates or discounts for reserving a block of rooms?

many hotels offer special rates or discounts for reserving a block of rooms for weddings or events. It's a good idea to inquire about this possibility when discussing your reservation with the hotel's event or group reservations department.


Q3: What if some of my guests have specific room preferences or require special accommodations?

It's important to communicate any special requests or accommodations needed by your guests to the hotel when setting up the room block. This can include preferences for room types (e.g., king, double, suite), accessibility requirements, or any other specific needs. The hotel will do its best to accommodate these requests, depending on availability.


Q4:What is the cancellation policy for the reserved rooms?

Hotel cancellation policies vary, so it's important to clarify this with the hotel when making the reservations. Ask about any penalties or fees associated with canceling or modifying room reservations, as well as the deadline for making changes without incurring charges


Q5:Can I request special amenities or services for my guests?

Yes, you can often request special amenities or services for your guests, such as welcome packages, room upgrades, or late check-out options. Discuss these requests with the hotel's event or sales team to see what options are available and if any additional fees apply.


Q6:Is there parking available for guests at the hotel?

Most hotels provide parking facilities for their guests. Inquire about the parking options, whether there are any fees associated with parking, and if there are any arrangements for valet parking.


Q7:Are there any nearby accommodations if the hotel block is full or if guests prefer other options?

It's a good idea to provide your guests with information about alternative accommodations in case the hotel block fills up or if guests prefer different options. Include details about nearby hotels, bed and breakfasts, or vacation rentals that they can consider.
